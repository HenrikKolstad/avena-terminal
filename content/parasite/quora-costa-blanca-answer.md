# Is it worth buying a new build in Costa Blanca in 2025?

**Short answer: yes, but only if you pick the right property in the right location. The spread between good deals and bad deals is enormous.**

I've spent the last six months analyzing new-build properties along Spain's coast, so I can give you a data-informed perspective rather than the usual agent pitch.

## The Case For

Costa Blanca has some genuine advantages right now. Developer competition means new builds are actually priced below comparable resale properties in many postcodes — the average discount I've measured is around 19%. That's unusual. Normally new builds carry a premium.

Rental yields are solid if you're in the right spot. Properties within walking distance of the beach in areas with good tourist footfall are achieving 7-8% gross yields. The 300+ days of sunshine, cheap flights from across Europe, and Spain's relatively low cost of living keep demand consistent.

You also get legal protections that are genuinely useful: a bank guarantee on your deposit during construction, a 10-year structural warranty, and clear regulations around the purchase process.

## The Case Against

The risks are real too. Some areas of Costa Blanca — particularly the southern stretch around Torrevieja and Orihuela Costa — have a lot of new-build inventory coming online. If you're buying purely for capital appreciation, oversupply could limit price growth in the medium term.

Completion delays are common. Budget an extra 3-6 months beyond the stated handover date. And the total purchase cost is 12-15% above the property price once you include VAT, stamp duty, legal fees, and setup costs.

Location quality varies enormously within a small area. A property 500 meters from the coast will significantly outperform one 3 kilometers inland, even if they're in the same municipality.

## My Recommendation

Don't buy based on a viewing trip and an agent's recommendation alone. Look at the data. Compare developments on objective metrics — price relative to comparables, yield estimates, developer track record, walkability.

I built a tool called [Avena Terminal](https://avenaterminal.com) specifically for this purpose. It scores new-build properties across Spain so you can see which ones actually offer value rather than just good marketing. It's free and might save you from an expensive mistake.

Costa Blanca in 2025 can be an excellent buy. But "Costa Blanca" is a 200-kilometer stretch of coastline, and the difference between a smart purchase and a mediocre one comes down to the specific property you choose.
