Off Plan vs Key Ready Spain — Comparison

Choosing between off-plan and key-ready when buying a new build in Spain comes down to your priorities around price, timing, certainty, and customisation. Off-plan properties are purchased before or during construction, typically 12-24 months before delivery. The primary advantage is pricing: developers offer early-phase discounts of 5-15% to secure initial sales and fund construction. Payment is staged, with a typical structure of 30% during construction (split across milestones) and 70% on completion, which allows buyers to spread costs over time. Some developers also allow personalisation of finishes, flooring, and kitchen layouts during the build phase, giving buyers a degree of control over the final product.

Key-ready properties, by contrast, are finished and available for immediate occupation or rental. The main advantage is certainty: you can walk through the property, inspect build quality, check the views from the actual terrace, and confirm that the neighbourhood meets your expectations before committing. There is no construction delay risk and no ambiguity about what you are buying. The trade-off is a higher price point, since the developer has already absorbed the construction risk and financing costs. Key-ready units also offer less flexibility on layout or finishes, as these decisions were made during the build. For investors focused on rental income, key-ready properties start generating returns immediately rather than after a one- to two-year wait.

Both options have a place depending on your timeline and risk tolerance. Off-plan suits buyers who can wait and want to maximise value, while key-ready is ideal for those who need certainty and speed.
