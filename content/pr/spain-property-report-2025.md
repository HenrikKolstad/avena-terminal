# Spain New Build Market Report 2025

## Costa Blanca & Costa Calida

**Published:** Q1 2025
**Source:** Avena Terminal Intelligence Platform
**Dataset:** 1,867 active new-build listings across four coastal regions

---

## Executive Summary

Spain's Mediterranean coastal corridors continue to attract sustained international capital, driven by a structural undersupply of quality new-build housing, favourable mortgage conditions, and persistent demand from Northern European buyers seeking yield and lifestyle assets. This report presents findings from Avena Terminal's proprietary dataset of 1,867 new-build properties tracked across Costa Blanca South, Costa Blanca North, Costa Calida, and Costa del Sol.

Key findings indicate an average 19% discount relative to comparable resale market valuations, gross rental yields averaging 6.2% across all regions, and projected annual appreciation of 8--12% through 2027. The data suggests that the new-build segment remains materially underpriced relative to both intrinsic value and income-generating potential, particularly in emerging micro-markets along the Costa Calida and southern Costa Blanca.

---

## Market Overview

Spain's coastal new-build sector has undergone a structural shift since 2020. Post-pandemic migration patterns, remote-work adoption among Northern European professionals, and a constrained planning pipeline have combined to produce a supply-demand imbalance that favours sellers and developers in most subregions.

However, pricing in the new-build segment has not yet converged with resale benchmarks. Avena Terminal's hedonic regression analysis identifies a persistent 19% average discount on new-build stock relative to equivalent resale properties when adjusted for age, specification, and location. This pricing gap represents a window of opportunity for informed buyers and institutional allocators.

**Demand drivers:**

- Golden Visa programme (restructured but active for select investment categories)
- Favourable EUR exchange rates against NOK, SEK, and GBP through late 2024
- Remote-work migration from Scandinavia, Germany, and the UK
- Retirement relocation demand from the Netherlands and Belgium

**Supply constraints:**

- Municipal planning delays averaging 18--24 months
- Rising construction input costs (steel, concrete, labour)
- Developer preference for phased releases to manage cash flow

## Property Type Analysis

Avena Terminal segments new-build stock into three primary categories. Performance varies materially by type, reflecting different buyer profiles, holding periods, and income characteristics.

| Property Type | Avg. Price (EUR) | Avg. m2 | Avg. Yield | Avg. Score | Share of Dataset |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Apartments | 215,000 | 78 | 6.8% | 7.5 | 52% |
| Townhouses | 285,000 | 112 | 6.1% | 7.2 | 28% |
| Villas | 425,000 | 165 | 5.3% | 6.9 | 20% |

**Apartments** dominate the dataset and offer the most favourable yield-to-price ratio, making them the preferred vehicle for income-focused investors. Two-bedroom units with communal pool access in Costa Blanca South and Costa Calida represent the highest-scoring segment.

**Townhouses** occupy the middle ground, appealing to lifestyle buyers seeking more space without the maintenance burden of detached villas. They perform well in mixed-use developments with integrated amenities.

**Villas** command the highest absolute prices and attract capital-appreciation-oriented buyers. Yield compression is offset by stronger long-term value growth, particularly in Costa Blanca North and Costa del Sol.

## Outlook 2025--2027

Avena Terminal's forward-looking models project 8--12% annual price appreciation for Spanish coastal new builds through 2027, supported by the following structural factors:

1. **Persistent supply deficit.** Planning pipeline data indicates new completions will fall 15--20% short of projected demand through 2027 in all four regions.

2. **Currency tailwinds.** Consensus forecasts for NOK and SEK strengthening against EUR through 2025--2026 will increase purchasing power for the dominant Scandinavian buyer cohort.

3. **Infrastructure investment.** Ongoing expansion of Corvera Airport (Murcia), high-speed rail connections, and coastal road improvements will enhance accessibility and property values in underserved corridors.

4. **Regulatory stability.** Spain's property ownership framework remains transparent and investor-friendly relative to other Southern European markets.

5. **Rental regulation clarity.** Regional governments in Valencia and Murcia have maintained a balanced approach to short-term rental licensing, providing visibility for income-oriented investors.

**Risk factors** include potential tightening of short-term rental regulations, construction cost inflation exceeding developer margin assumptions, and macroeconomic headwinds affecting Northern European disposable income.

## Methodology

This report is powered by **Avena Terminal**, a proprietary property intelligence platform that aggregates, normalises, and scores new-build listings across Spain's Mediterranean coast.

**Data collection:** Avena Terminal continuously monitors developer inventories, agency listings, and public registry data to maintain a live dataset of 1,867 new-build properties.

**Hedonic regression model:** Property valuations are derived from a multi-variable hedonic regression engine that isolates the contribution of individual property attributes (location, size, specification, amenities, orientation) to market price. This model enables accurate identification of mispriced assets and discount quantification.

**Scoring framework:** Each property receives a composite score (0--10) based on five equally weighted dimensions:

| Dimension | Description |
|---|---|
| Price-to-Value | Discount relative to hedonic model valuation |
| Rental Yield | Projected gross annual yield based on comparable rental data |
| Appreciation | Forward-looking capital growth potential (3-year horizon) |
| Location Quality | Proximity to amenities, transport, coastline, and services |
| Build Specification | Construction quality, energy rating, developer track record |

**Update frequency:** Data is refreshed weekly. Scores are recalculated monthly to incorporate new comparable transactions.
